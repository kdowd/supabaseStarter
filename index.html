The sinking of the RMS Titanic on April 15, 1912, killed approximately 1,500 people, roughly 68% of the 2,200+ passengers and crew on board.
Most victims died from hypothermia in the freezing North Atlantic waters, with only 337 bodies recovered. Major casualties included crew members and third-class passengers, who had lower survival rates.
